## Objective

Route the `mark_status` write leg through the kind-aware authority that its own commit leg and `move_task` validation already use (fixes #2154), route the two mixed-partition auto-commit bundles through the `BookkeepingTransaction` pattern (fixes #2155), route the 6 canon sites in these two files through `_canonicalize_primary_read_handle`, and shrink the WP01 baseline allowlist to reflect the routed sites. ## Context

Both defects share a root: callers bypass the existing resolution authority rather than the authority being absent. `mark_status`'s write leg (`tasks.py:1807`) resolves via the kind-blind `resolve_feature_dir_for_mission`, while its commit leg (`:1906`) and `move_task`'s validation (`:658`) correctly use `resolve_planning_read_dir(kind=TASKS_INDEX)` pointing at the primary surface. Result: the write lands on coord, the validator reads primary, every WP blocks on phantom "unchecked subtasks" (#2154).

The two mixed-bundle callers (`tasks.py:1555` `move_task` auto-commit and `implement.py:1311` claim auto-commit) bundle a primary-surface WP file with coord-owned status artifacts (`status.events.jsonl`, `status.json`) in a single primary-worktree commit. The `safe_commit` guard at `git/commit_helpers.py:983-991` correctly refuses the coord-worktree paths — and both callers silently swallow the `SafeCommitPathPolicyError` as an "Auto-commit skipped" warning, leaving the feature branch dirty (#2155). The guard is not the defect; the callers are.

See `spec.md` FR-001/FR-002, `contracts/resolution-gates.md` Contract 3/Contract 4, and `plan.md` IC-04a/IC-04b for the full design rationale. The `BookkeepingTransaction` pattern in `src/specify_cli/agent/workflow.py:_commit_workflow_change` is the model to copy for the bundle split.
